What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing your website so that AI-powered search engines — including ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude, and Gemini — can discover, understand, and cite your content. Unlike traditional SEO, which optimizes for ranked lists of blue links, GEO optimizes for being selected as a cited source in AI-generated answers.

The shift is fundamental. In 2026, more than 40% of search sessions on Google now end without a click, and a growing share of queries never reach Google at all — users go directly to ChatGPT or Perplexity. For content creators, SaaS founders, and publishers, this means a new discipline is required. Your site needs to be readable to AI crawlers (robots.txt, llms.txt), machine-parseable (JSON-LD schema), and authoritative (E-E-A-T signals, third-party citations).

GeoScore measures 12 dimensions of this readiness: AI crawlability (which crawlers can access your site), AI guidance (whether you provide a curated llms.txt), structured data (JSON-LD coverage and quality), meta and social signals, content quality and depth, E-E-A-T signals (author bylines, trust pages, reviews), brand and entity presence, citation readiness (external sources, expert quotes, .edu/.gov links), discovery (sitemap, AI-specific endpoints like /ai/summary.json), agent-friendliness (API documentation, structured outputs), and content freshness.

Each audit produces a 0–100 score, a radar chart of the 12 dimensions, and a prioritized list of fixes. The tool also auto-generates three fix files you can deploy immediately: a production-ready llms.txt, an AI-crawler-friendly robots.txt, and a JSON-LD snippet for Organization, Article, FAQ, and HowTo schema. The audit runs entirely client-side and your URLs are not stored.

If your site scores below 60, the most common cause is robots.txt blocking AI crawlers by default. Adding GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Google-Extended to the allow list, and shipping an llms.txt, typically raises the score by 20–30 points in a single deployment. The highest-scoring sites combine technical compliance with content depth, source citations, and structured data that AI models can parse in one pass.
